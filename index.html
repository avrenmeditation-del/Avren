01 The premise

Most apps hand you a library. Avren hands you a sound.

✦
A tonal

One sound, yours

On your first sit you're given a tonal — a single phonetic sound, held in the back of the breath. Never said aloud. Yours for the whole practice.

☾
A guide

A voice beside you

One of three — Kael, Seren, or Ryn. They say the tonal once, then leave you to the silence. The same voice sits with you across months.

✧
A few minutes

Five, ten, fifteen

The practice asks only what the day allows. You sit, briefly, most days — and the rest takes care of itself over time.

02 Why it's not another meditation app
iNo catalogue

Nothing to choose from

No endless menu of sleep stories, soundscapes, and 47 courses you'll never finish. One practice. You open it, you sit, you close it. Decision fatigue is not relaxing.

iiNo guilt mechanics

A streak you can't lose in a night

We count Days Kept, with two grace days built in. Miss a day and nothing shatters. A practice you're afraid to break isn't a practice — it's a debt.

iiiIt grows

The orb evolves as you do

Your tonal lives as a small, breathing orb. With each sit it gathers rings, orbiters, light — a quiet, honest record of practice rather than a number on a card.

ivYour inner weather

One word, after each sit

Afterward you give a single word — scattered, wide, still. Over weeks it draws a weather log of your inner climate. No charts to optimize. Just something true to look back on.

03 · The Temple

A record of practice, not performance.

Most apps reward you with badges and confetti. Avren just lets your orb become more itself — a halo here, an orbiting mote there — as the days accumulate. Nothing is unlocked. Nothing is bought. It simply reflects the time you've spent in the quiet.

06 Before you ask

Fair question. The tonal draws on the same lineage as mantra-based practice, yes — but Avren isn't a library of techniques to graze. It's one sound, one voice, and a structure built to keep you returning. The difference is less about the sound and more about everything we left out.

None. Your guide introduces everything on the first sit and then gets out of the way. If you can sit and breathe for five minutes, you can do this. Most people start at five and never feel pressure to go longer.

No doctrine, no belief required. The atmosphere is intentionally quiet and a little ceremonial because that's what helps people slow down — not because we're asking you to subscribe to anything. Bring whatever you believe, or nothing at all.

The waitlist is free. At launch Avren will be a paid subscription — we'd rather charge a fair price than sell your attention or your data. Fold I members get founding pricing and a say in how it grows. Exact numbers will be in your invitation, with no surprises. And cost should never be the reason someone can't practice — there's a Temple Scholarship: a no-questions-asked free pass for anyone who needs it.

Fold I — the first hundred — opens soon. We're keeping the first group small on purpose so we can sit with real people and get it right before opening wider. Join the list and you'll be among the first contacted.

It sits on the waitlist and nowhere else. One note when your fold opens, the occasional quiet update, and an unsubscribe link on every one. No selling, no list-trading, no daily nudges.
