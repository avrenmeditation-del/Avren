01 The premise

Most apps hand you a library. Avren hands you a sound.

✦
Your tonal

One sound, yours

On your first session you're given a tonal: a single phonetic sound to repeat silently as you sit. You never say it aloud. It stays yours for the whole practice.

☾
Your guide

A voice beside you

One of three: Kael, Seren, or Ryn. They speak your tonal once, then leave you to the silence. The same voice stays with you across months, and remembers where you left off.

✧
Your time

Five to twenty

Five, ten, fifteen, twenty. Sit for as long as the day allows. You show up briefly, most days, and the practice does the rest over time.

02 Why it's not another meditation app
iNo catalogue

Nothing to choose from

No endless menu of sleep stories, soundscapes, and 47 courses you'll never finish. You open it, you sit, you close it. Decision fatigue is not relaxing.

iiNo guilt mechanics

A streak you can't lose in a night

We count the days you showed up, with two grace days built in. Miss a day, a week, or a month, and your guide simply meets you where you are when you come back. A practice you're afraid to break isn't a practice. It's a debt.

iiiIt grows

The orb evolves as you do

Your tonal lives as a small, breathing orb. With each session it gathers rings, orbiters, and light. It's a quiet, honest record of your practice rather than a number on a card.

ivYour inner weather

One word, after each session

Afterward you give a single word. Scattered. Wide. Still. Whatever you offer is the right answer; it's simply what your guide listens for. Over weeks the words become a weather log of your inner climate. Nothing to optimize. Just something true to look back on.

The orb grows as your focus deepens.

Your progress isn't points or badges built to keep you hooked. The orb is a quiet reflection of the time you choose to set aside, and nothing else asks for your attention.

03 Questions

A tonal is a single phonetic sound, like a personal note assigned just to you. You repeat it silently and effortlessly as you sit. You never say it aloud or concentrate hard on it. It's a place for the mind to rest, and it fades on its own as you settle.

Avren teaches one technique: effortless, silent repetition of your tonal. There's no menu of styles to choose between, and nothing to get right beyond sitting down. If you want a single simple practice rather than a toolkit of exercises, this is built for you. Avren is a meditation practice, not a medical or therapeutic tool, and isn't a substitute for professional care.

Avren is $11.99 per month, or $99 per year, which works out to about 30% less than paying monthly. Every subscription starts with a free trial, so you can try the practice before you pay.

If the cost is a barrier, the Temple Scholarship gives you six months free, with no proof and no questions asked. Just send us a short note and we'll set you up. We never want the price to be the reason you can't practice.

We'd rather get the practice right for a smaller number of people than rush it to everyone. Early access lets us listen closely, shape it carefully, and keep the experience calm and considered.
